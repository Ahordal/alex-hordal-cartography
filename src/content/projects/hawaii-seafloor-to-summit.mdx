This project involved conceptualizing, designing, and developing a topobathymetric map of the Hawaiian Islands that emphasizes overall vertical structure, titled *Hawaiʻi: Seafloor to Summit*.

The process began with a design sketch that identified the major components of the proposed map and established a rough layout. From there, the focus shifted to planning and collecting the necessary data. The datasets included three topobathymetric sources:
- NOAA ETOPO 2022
- Coastal Relief Model
- SOEST Hawaiʻi Mapping Research Group multibeam data

The DEMs were resampled to a consistent 90 m resolution and merged into a single continuous topobathymetric DEM, along with supporting vector datasets from the Hawaiʻi Statewide GIS Program. 

A key design consideration was the symbolization of the merged DEM using a custom continuous, diverging colour ramp that seamlessly blended topography and bathymetry at mean sea level. An 8.5× vertically exaggerated elevation profile of Mauna Kea was also included to reinforce the seafloor-to-summit narrative.

Bathymetric and terrestrial features were identified and labelled, including:
- points of interest
- population centres
- summits
- volcanoes
- seamounts
- underwater landslides

Small contextual snippets were also incorporated to highlight major geographic features throughout the composition.

## Applied Skills

- Applied raster processing techniques to resample and merge multiple DEM datasets into a continuous topobathymetric surface.
- Designed and implemented a custom diverging colour ramp to effectively communicate elevation change across terrestrial and bathymetric environments.
- Applied cartographic design principles to develop a clear and visually balanced map layout supporting the seafloor-to-summit narrative.
- Produced a topobathymetric map that communicates complex elevation relationships through effective visualization and supporting contextual elements.
- Extracted elevation data from DEMs and designed a vertically exaggerated elevation profile of Mauna Kea to effectively communicate its full seafloor-to-summit extent.
